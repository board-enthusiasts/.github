# Board Enthusiasts Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

Board Enthusiasts (BE) is committed to fostering a welcoming, respectful, and constructive environment for everyone who participates in our community and projects.

We want BE spaces to be places where people can collaborate, learn, share feedback, and build useful things without fear of harassment, hostility, or personal attacks.

This Code of Conduct applies to participation in BE GitHub repositories, discussions, issues, pull requests, project spaces, and other official BE community spaces where this Code of Conduct is referenced.

## Expected Behavior

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment include:

- Being respectful and considerate in communication
- Assuming good intent where reasonable
- Giving constructive, specific, and actionable feedback
- Focusing on ideas, implementations, and outcomes rather than attacking people
- Being open to different viewpoints, experience levels, and backgrounds
- Accepting feedback gracefully
- Helping maintain a community that is useful, trustworthy, and safe for others

## Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, intimidation, stalking, or threatening behavior
- Personal attacks, insults, or hostile commentary
- Trolling, baiting, flamebait, or deliberately provocative behavior
- Defamatory statements or knowingly false accusations about individuals, teams, companies, or products
- Abusive, hateful, discriminatory, or demeaning language
- Repeated bad-faith argumentation or attempts to derail discussion
- Publishing or threatening to publish private, confidential, or identifying information about others without permission
- Encouraging or coordinating harassment of any person or group
- Impersonation or misrepresentation intended to deceive or harm others
- Any other conduct that a BE maintainer reasonably determines is intended to harm, intimidate, demean, or poison the community environment

## Respect Toward Board, Harris Hill, and Related Parties

Board Enthusiasts is an independent community effort. We expect discussion involving the Board platform and related people or entities to remain respectful and constructive.

Participants must not use BE spaces to harass, defame, target, or otherwise maliciously disparage:

- the Board team
- Harris Hill
- any of their team members
- any of their products, services, or related offerings

Constructive criticism, disagreement, product feedback, and good-faith discussion are allowed. However, they must be expressed professionally and without personal attacks, harassment, defamation, ridicule campaigns, or hostile dogpiling.

This means, for example:

- **Allowed:** “I think this product decision creates friction for developers because...”
- **Not allowed:** personal attacks, smear-style claims, insulting language, harassment, or attempts to rally hostility against specific people or organizations

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all BE-managed GitHub spaces, including:

- repositories
- issues
- pull requests
- discussions
- project boards
- wiki or documentation collaboration spaces
- comments and review threads

BE maintainers may also consider behavior outside these spaces if it is clearly targeted, severe, and reasonably likely to harm the safety, participation, or integrity of the BE community.

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, please report it to the BE maintainers.

If a repository or organization-level support or security document provides a specific reporting path, please use that path. Otherwise, contact the maintainers through the designated BE support channel.

When making a report, please include, if possible:

- what happened
- where it happened
- links or screenshots
- who was involved
- any relevant context

We will review reports in good faith and handle them as responsibly and discreetly as reasonably possible.

## Enforcement

BE maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers may take any action they deem appropriate in response to behavior that violates this Code of Conduct, including but not limited to:

- requesting a change in behavior
- editing, hiding, locking, or removing content
- closing discussions or pull requests
- removing access to community spaces
- temporary suspension from participation
- permanent ban from BE-managed spaces

Enforcement decisions may be made at the maintainers’ discretion based on the severity, pattern, and context of the behavior.

## Good-Faith Participation

Not every disagreement is a violation.

We welcome respectful critique, differing opinions, and honest feedback. The standard is not “always agree.” The standard is to participate in a way that is constructive, respectful, and not abusive, defamatory, or malicious.

## Changes

BE may update this Code of Conduct from time to time to better support the health, safety, and sustainability of the community.
